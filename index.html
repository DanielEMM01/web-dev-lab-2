Tres Leches (Milk Cake)

Recipe source: Allrecipes. Tres Leches (Milk Cake)

Description/Story:

Tres Leches Cake is a light sponge cake that is soaked in a mixture of three different types of milk, which gives it a very moist and rich texture. The name “tres leches” means “three milks” in Spanish, and this dessert is especially popular in many Latin American countries. Even though the cake absorbs a lot of liquid, it doesn't become soggy. Instead, it stays soft and airy while being full of sweet and creamy flavor.

What makes this dessert interesting is the contrast between the fluffy cake and the milk mixture that soaks into it. After chilling, the flavors blend together and the texture becomes even better. The whipped cream topping adds a smooth and light finish that balances the sweetness of the cake and makes it look appealing when served.

I chose this recipe because it represents a cultural dessert that is both simple and impressive. Although it's a Mexican dessert, it has also become popular in Venezuela where I am from and all over South America. It's perfect for parties, gatherings, or special occasions because it can be made ahead of time and served cold. I also like that it introduces a cultural element, showing how different regions have their own classic desserts that use basic ingredients in creative ways.

Ingredients and Amounts:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (12 ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Gather the ingredients
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour a 9x13 inch baking pan.
  3. Sift flour and baking powder together; set aside.
  4. Beat sugar and butter together in a large bowl with an electric mixer until lighter in color and fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla; beat well. Add flour mixture, about 1/2 cup at a time, mixing until well blended.
  5. Pour batter into the prepared baking pan.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, about 30 minutes. Pierce cake all over with a fork; let cool to room temperature.
  7. Mix whole milk, sweetened condensed milk, and evaporated milk together in a bowl.
  8. Pour the whole milk, condensed milk, and evaporated milk mixture over the top of the cooled cake and allow to soak in.
  9. Whip cream, remaining 1 cup of sugar, and 1 teaspoon vanilla in a chilled glass or metal bowl with an electric mixer until thick.
  10. When milk mixture has soaked in, spread cream on top of the cake.
  11. Keep cake refrigerated until ready to serve. Sprinkle ground cinnamon over top of the cake before slicing. Enjoy!
